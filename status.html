Marine Species Sustainability

Biological Overview

Salmon belong to the family Salmonidae, primarily in the genera Salmo (Atlantic) and Oncorhynchus (Pacific). Close relatives include trout, char, and grayling.

Anadromy

Most salmon are anadromous—hatching in freshwater, migrating to the ocean, and returning to natal streams to spawn. Some populations are landlocked.

Homing Instinct

They return to their birthplace to spawn, guided by olfactory memory. Straying occurs but is species-dependent.

Species of Salmon

Atlantic Salmon (Salmo salar)

Found in the North Atlantic; some populations are landlocked (e.g., in North America and Europe).

Pacific Salmon (Oncorhynchus spp.)

  • Chinook/King: Largest species.
  • Sockeye/Red: Known for plankton-based diet.
  • Coho/Silver: Coastal range from Alaska to California.
  • Pink/Humpback: Smallest and most abundant.
  • Chum/Dog: Widest geographic range.
  • Masu/Cherry: Native to East Asia.

Life Cycle

  1. Eggs: Laid in freshwater gravel beds.
  2. Alevin/Fry: Emerge with yolk sacs, develop into parr (camouflaged juveniles).
  3. Smoltification: Physiological changes allow transition to saltwater.
  4. Ocean Phase: Spend 1–5 years feeding.
  5. Spawning Migration: Return to natal streams; most die after spawning (semelparity), though some Atlantic salmon survive to spawn again.

Ecological Role

Salmon are keystone species that transport nutrients from oceans to freshwater ecosystems, supporting forests, bears, and birds.

Ecosystem Engineers

  • Bears: Spread nutrients via uneaten carcasses.
  • Beavers: Create habitats benefiting juvenile salmon.
  • Lampreys: Contribute to nutrient cycling and serve as alternative prey.

Human Interaction

Commercial Fishing

Wild Pacific salmon dominate catches; Atlantic salmon are mostly farmed. Global wild catch has stabilized at ~1 million tonnes/year.

Aquaculture

  • Farmed Salmon: Major industry in Norway, Chile, and Scotland. Issues include sea lice, disease, and reliance on wild fish for feed.
  • Alternatives: Plant-based feeds, seaweed, and yeast biomass are being explored.

Conservation

Many wild stocks are declining due to dams, habitat loss, and climate change. Hatcheries and spawning channels aid recovery.

Cultural Significance

Indigenous Peoples

Salmon are central to diets, ceremonies, and art (e.g., Pacific Northwest tribes like the Haida).

Mythology

  • Celtic: Salmon of Knowledge (Ireland), Salmon of Llyn Llyw (Wales).
  • Norse: Loki transforms into a salmon to escape the gods.

Recreational Fishing

Highly valued sport fish, with economic and cultural importance.

Pacific Sardines

Rebuilding Population Fishery Closed

Scientific name: Sardinops sagax caerulea

Population Status

The Pacific sardine population is not overfished but is still rebuilding. The fishery is currently closed as the stock is below the precautionary level set by managers.

Biology

  • Small fish with blue-green backs and white flanks
  • Can grow to more than 12 inches long
  • Live up to 13 years (usually not past 5)
  • Reproduce at age 1 or 2
  • Spawn multiple times per season

Habitat

Found from southeastern Alaska to Baja California, Mexico. They form large schools near the ocean surface.

Shellfish Aquaculture

Highly Sustainable

What is Shellfish Aquaculture?

Shellfish aquaculture involves breeding, raising, and harvesting shellfish like oysters, clams, mussels, and scallops.

Types of Shellfish

  • Oysters: Grown in racks or cages
  • Clams: Littleneck and Manila clams
  • Mussels: Farmed using rope systems
  • Scallops: Raised using hanging techniques

Benefits

  • Improve water quality by filtering
  • Create habitat for other marine life
  • Require no feed or fertilizers

Mahi-Mahi (Dolphinfish)

Sustainable Choice

Scientific name: Coryphaena hippurus

Habitat

Found in warm oceanic waters worldwide, especially near floating debris or seaweed.

Interesting Facts

  • Name means "very strong" in Hawaiian
  • One of the fastest-growing fish
  • Can swim up to 57 mph
  • Change colors when caught

Fishing

Popular for sport fishing. Best caught with pole-and-line for sustainability.
